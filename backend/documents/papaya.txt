=== PAPAYA CROP GUIDE ===

1. Fertilizers:
Papaya requires balanced nutrient applications. Recommended dose is 100:100:200 kg of N:P:K per acre.
- Apply NPK in monthly splits starting from the second month after transplanting.
- Incorporate well-rotted farmyard manure (FYM) and vermicompost at planting.
- Spray Borax (0.1%) during flowering to prevent fruit cracking.

2. Irrigation:
Papaya irrigation and water requirements: Moderate to high water requirements. Irrigate every 8-10 days in winter and 6-8 days in summer. Drip irrigation is ideal. Avoid water contact with the stem trunk to prevent stem rot.

3. Pest Management:
Papaya pest management: Spider Mites, Whiteflies, and Aphids. Mites cause yellowing and dry webbing under leaves. Spray wettable sulphur (3g/L) for mite control.

4. Growth Stages:
Papaya has the following stage cycle durations:
- Vegetative Stage: Sowing up to 60 days.
- Flowering Stage: From 60 days to 150 days.
- Fruiting Stage: From 150 days to 210 days.
- Harvesting Stage: From 210 days onwards.

5. Harvesting:
Papaya harvesting and post-harvest practices: Harvest fruits when they show yellow streaks on the green skin (color break stage). Cut with a sharp knife leaving a short stem. Store at 10-12 degrees Celsius.

6. Diseases:
Papaya diseases: Papaya Ring Spot Virus (PRSV), Damping-off, and Foot Rot. Ring Spot Virus is transmitted by aphids, causing mosaic symptoms. Control aphid vectors using yellow sticky traps and insecticidal soap sprays.

7. Soil Requirements:
Papaya soil requirements: Well-drained sandy loam or alluvial soil with pH between 6.0 and 6.5. Must have excellent drainage; water logging for even 24 hours can kill the root system.
