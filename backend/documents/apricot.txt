=== APRICOT CROP GUIDE ===

1. Fertilizers:
Apricot requires balanced nutrient applications. Recommended dose is 60:40:60 kg of N:P:K per acre (or per tree equivalent).
Category: Fruit Crops
- Apply organic compost and full Phosphorus/Potassium as basal post-harvest pruning.
- Apply Nitrogen in splits: pre-flowering and post-fruit set.
- Foliar spray Zinc and Boron before flowering to improve fruit set and reduce cracking.

2. Irrigation:
Apricot irrigation and water requirements: Moderate to high water requirement. Regular drip irrigation is highly recommended. Avoid water stress during flowering.

3. Pest Management:
Apricot pest management: Common pests affecting Apricot are Fruit Fly, Mealybugs, Thrips. Use yellow sticky traps, pheromone traps, and spray spinosad or neem oil.

4. Growth Stages:
Apricot has the following stage cycle durations:
- Vegetative Stage: Sowing up to 60 days.
- Flowering Stage: From 60 days to 120 days.
- Fruiting Stage: From 120 days to 210 days.
- Harvesting Stage: From 210 days onwards.

5. Harvesting:
Apricot harvesting and post-harvest practices: For Apricot, Harvest when fruits achieve mature size and color break stage. Handle gently to prevent bruising.

6. Diseases:
Apricot diseases: Common diseases affecting Apricot are Anthracnose, Powdery Mildew, Canker. Prune affected branches and spray copper-based fungicides.

7. Soil Requirements:
Apricot soil requirements: Requires deep, well-drained loamy or clay loam soil rich in organic humus. Optimal pH is 5.5 to 7.5.
