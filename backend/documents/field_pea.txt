=== FIELD PEA CROP GUIDE ===

1. Fertilizers:
Field Pea requires balanced nutrient applications. Recommended dose is 10:20:10 kg of N:P:K per acre.
Category: Pulses
- Apply all NPK as a basal dose during sowing.
- Inoculate seeds with Rhizobium culture before sowing to enhance nitrogen fixation.
- Apply secondary nutrients (Sulphur) to increase seed yield.

2. Irrigation:
Field Pea irrigation and water requirements: Low water requirement. Drought-resilient. Irrigate at flowering and pod development stages.

3. Pest Management:
Field Pea pest management: Common pests affecting Field Pea are Pod Borer, Aphids, Whitefly. Use pheromone traps and spray spinosad or neem formulations.

4. Growth Stages:
Field Pea has the following stage cycle durations:
- Vegetative Stage: Sowing up to 30 days.
- Flowering Stage: From 30 days to 60 days.
- Fruiting Stage: From 60 days to 90 days.
- Harvesting Stage: From 90 days onwards.

5. Harvesting:
Field Pea harvesting and post-harvest practices: For Field Pea, Harvest when pods turn dry, yellow-brown. Hand pull or cut plants, dry in sun, and thresh.

6. Diseases:
Field Pea diseases: Common diseases affecting Field Pea are Wilt, Root Rot, Powdery Mildew. Spray broad-spectrum fungicides and use resistant varieties.

7. Soil Requirements:
Field Pea soil requirements: Requires well-drained loamy or sandy loam soil. Optimal soil pH is 6.0 to 7.5. Sensitive to salinity.
