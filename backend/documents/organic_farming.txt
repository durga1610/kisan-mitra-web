Organic Farming: Soil Enrichment Methods
Composting: The biological decomposition of organic waste (crop residue, animal dung, kitchen waste) under aerobic conditions. Composting adds humus, improves soil structure, water holding capacity, and microbial activity.
Vermicomposting: Using earthworms (especially Eisenia fetida) to convert organic waste into high-quality nutrient-rich manure. Vermicompost is rich in growth hormones, micro-nutrients, and beneficial microflora.
Green Manuring: Growing quick-growing leguminous crops (like Sunnhemp, Dhaincha, or Cowpea) and ploughing them directly back into the soil at the flowering stage. This adds significant nitrogen and organic matter.
Biofertilizers: Living microorganisms like Azotobacter, Rhizobium (for legumes), and Phosphate Solubilizing Bacteria (PSB) that help fix atmospheric nitrogen or solubilize phosphorus in the soil.

Organic Farming: Pest and Disease Control
Neem-Based Pesticides: Neem Seed Kernel Extract (NSKE 5%) or Neem Oil sprays act as powerful insect growth regulators, antifeedants, and repellents for sucking pests and caterpillars.
Dashaparni Ark: An organic pesticide made by fermenting ten different native plant leaves (such as neem, papaya, castor, dhatura) with cow dung and urine. Highly effective against chewing and sucking pests.
Trichoderma viride: A beneficial bio-fungicide that controls soil-borne root diseases (root rot, wilt, damping-off) by parasitizing harmful fungal pathogens. Apply mixed with well-rotted FYM.
Pseudomonas fluorescens: A bacterial bio-control agent that controls leaf spots, blights, and rusts when sprayed on foliage or used for seed treatment.
