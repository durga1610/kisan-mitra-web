=== KODO MILLET CROP GUIDE ===

1. Fertilizers:
Kodo Millet requires balanced nutrient applications. Recommended dose is 40:20:20 kg of N:P:K per acre.
Category: Cereals
- Apply all Phosphorus and Potassium and half Nitrogen as basal dose during field preparation.
- Apply remaining Nitrogen in splits during the active growth and tillering phases.
- Supplement with organic compost or farmyard manure (FYM) to improve soil structure.

2. Irrigation:
Kodo Millet irrigation and water requirements: Moderate water requirement. Irrigate regularly during critical stages like tillering, flowering and grain filling.

3. Pest Management:
Kodo Millet pest management: Common pests affecting Kodo Millet are Stem Borer, Shoot Fly, Aphids. Manage by installing pheromone traps and spraying organic neem oil formulations.

4. Growth Stages:
Kodo Millet has the following stage cycle durations:
- Vegetative Stage: Sowing up to 35 days.
- Flowering Stage: From 35 days to 75 days.
- Fruiting Stage: From 75 days to 105 days.
- Harvesting Stage: From 105 days onwards.

5. Harvesting:
Kodo Millet harvesting and post-harvest practices: For Kodo Millet, Harvest when leaves dry and stalks turn brown. Thresh, clean, and dry grains below 12% moisture for storage.

6. Diseases:
Kodo Millet diseases: Common diseases affecting Kodo Millet are Rust, Blast, Smut. Spray systemic fungicides or remove infected parts to prevent spread.

7. Soil Requirements:
Kodo Millet soil requirements: Requires well-drained alluvial, loamy or clayey loam soil. Optimal soil pH is 6.0 to 7.5.
