=== ONION CROP GUIDE ===

1. Fertilizers:
Onion requires balanced nutrient applications. Recommended dose is 40:20:30 kg of N:P:K per acre.
Category: Spices
- Apply half Nitrogen and full Phosphorus and Potassium as basal.
- Apply remaining Nitrogen 30 days after transplanting.
- Apply Sulphur (15kg/acre) to improve onion bulb pungency and quality.

2. Irrigation:
Onion irrigation and water requirements: Moderate water requirement. Needs regular irrigation every 7-10 days. Stop irrigation 10-15 days before harvesting to ensure long shelf life.

3. Pest Management:
Onion pest management: Onion Thrips are the primary pest, causing silver streaks on leaves. Control with yellow sticky traps and neem oil sprays.

4. Growth Stages:
Onion has the following stage cycle durations:
- Vegetative Stage: Sowing up to 40 days.
- Flowering Stage: From 40 days to 90 days.
- Fruiting Stage: From 90 days to 120 days.
- Harvesting Stage: From 120 days onwards.

5. Harvesting:
Onion harvesting and post-harvest practices: Harvest when 50-70% of the crop canopy falls over (neck fall stage). Pull out bulbs, cure in shade for 3-5 days, and trim tops before storing.

6. Diseases:
Onion diseases: Purple Blotch and Downy Mildew. Purple blotch causes purple concentric lesions on leaves. Spray mancozeb or copper oxychloride.

7. Soil Requirements:
Onion soil requirements: Sandy loam or alluvial loamy soil with pH 6.0 to 7.0. Avoid heavy clay or stony soils that restrict bulb expansion.
