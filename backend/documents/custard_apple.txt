=== CUSTARD APPLE CROP GUIDE ===

1. Fertilizers:
Custard Apple requires balanced nutrient applications. Recommended dose is 30:30:30 kg of N:P:K per acre.
- Apply all Phosphorus and Potassium and half Nitrogen as basal dose during field preparation.
- Apply remaining Nitrogen in splits during the active growth and tillering phases.
- Supplement with organic compost or farmyard manure (FYM) to improve soil structure.

2. Irrigation:
Custard Apple irrigation and water requirements: Low water requirement. Irrigate regularly during critical stages like flowering and pod development, avoiding waterlogging.

3. Pest Management:
Custard Apple pest management: Common pests are Mealybugs, Scale Insects. Manage by installing pheromone traps and spraying organic neem oil formulations.

4. Growth Stages:
Custard Apple has the following stage cycle durations:
- Vegetative Stage: Sowing up to 50 days.
- Flowering Stage: From 50 days to 100 days.
- Fruiting Stage: From 100 days to 150 days.
- Harvesting Stage: From 150 days onwards.

5. Harvesting:
Custard Apple harvesting and post-harvest practices: Harvest when leaves dry and stalks turn brown. Thresh, clean, and dry grains or fruits to safe moisture levels (below 12%) before packaging.

6. Diseases:
Custard Apple diseases: Common diseases affecting Custard Apple are Anthracnose. Spray systemic fungicides or remove infected parts to prevent spread.

7. Soil Requirements:
Custard Apple soil requirements: Requires well-drained sandy loam soil. Optimal soil pH is 6.0 to 7.5.
