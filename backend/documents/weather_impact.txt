Weather Impact: Thermal and Heat Stress
High Temperature Impact: Extremely high temperatures (above 35°C) increase evapotranspiration, cause wilting, leaf scorch, blossom drop, and poor pollination in crops like Tomato, Cotton, and Maize.
Mitigation: Irrigate crops during early morning or late evening hours to prevent water loss. Use mulches (organic straw or plastic) to keep soil temperature cool and preserve moisture. Set up windbreaks or shade nets for sensitive crops.

Weather Impact: Frost and Cold Stress
Frost Impact: Sudden drop in temperature to sub-zero levels causes water inside plant cells to freeze, leading to cell rupture, blackening, and death of tender leaves, shoots, and flowers, especially in Potato, Tomato, and Mustard.
Mitigation: Irrigate the field lightly on the evening preceding the expected frost (wet soil holds more heat than dry soil). Set up smoke fires (smudging) at borders to raise ambient temperature. Apply straw cover or plastic mulches.

Weather Impact: Heavy Rain and Waterlogging
Waterlogging Impact: Prolonged saturated soils lack oxygen, causing roots to suffocate, rot, and fail to absorb nutrients. Leaf yellowing, wilting, and plant death follow. Fungal diseases spread rapidly.
Mitigation: Maintain a robust field drainage system with trenches to direct excess water away. Plant crops on raised beds or ridges (such as Ridge planting for Cotton and Potato). Spray foliar nitrogen after drainage to restore plant energy.

Weather Impact: High Humidity and Monsoon Disease Preparation
Monsoon Prep: High humidity combined with warm temperatures creates the perfect environment for fungal and bacterial spores to germinate and spread (e.g., late blight, downy mildew, leaf spots, blast).
Mitigation: Space plants appropriately to ensure air circulation and sunlight penetration. Prune lower diseased leaves. Spray preventive copper oxychloride or organic bio-fungicides like Trichoderma prior to monsoon onset.
