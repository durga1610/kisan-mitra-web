=== TARRAGON CROP GUIDE ===

1. Fertilizers:
Tarragon requires balanced nutrient applications. Recommended dose is 30:20:30 kg of N:P:K per acre.
Category: Spices
- Apply full Phosphorus, Potassium and half Nitrogen basal during field preparation.
- Apply remaining Nitrogen in splits at 30, 60, and 90 days after planting.
- Apply Sulphur to enhance flavor, pungency, and compound concentration.

2. Irrigation:
Tarragon irrigation and water requirements: Moderate water requirement. Water every 7-10 days depending on dry spells. Avoid waterlogging at root zones.

3. Pest Management:
Tarragon pest management: Common pests affecting Tarragon are Thrips, Shoot Borer, Rhizome Scale. Control with yellow sticky traps and spraying neem oil.

4. Growth Stages:
Tarragon has the following stage cycle durations:
- Vegetative Stage: Sowing up to 45 days.
- Flowering Stage: From 45 days to 90 days.
- Fruiting Stage: From 90 days to 130 days.
- Harvesting Stage: From 130 days onwards.

5. Harvesting:
Tarragon harvesting and post-harvest practices: For Tarragon, Harvest when leaves turn yellow-brown and start drying. Dig rhizomes/bulbs carefully and cure in shade.

6. Diseases:
Tarragon diseases: Common diseases affecting Tarragon are Rhizome Rot, Leaf Spot, Blight. Drench soil with copper fungicides; ensure proper field drainage.

7. Soil Requirements:
Tarragon soil requirements: Requires rich, well-drained sandy loam or clay loam soil with high organic content. Optimal pH is 6.0 to 7.0.
