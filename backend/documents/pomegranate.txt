=== POMEGRANATE CROP GUIDE ===

1. Fertilizers:
Pomegranate requires balanced nutrient applications. Recommended dose is 50:25:25 kg of N:P:K per acre.
- Apply Nitrogen and Potassium in split doses during bahar treatment (flowering induction).
- Apply full Phosphorus dose basal during pruning.
- Apply micronutrients (Zinc and Boron) to reduce fruit splitting.

2. Irrigation:
Pomegranate irrigation and water requirements: Low to moderate water requirement. Regular drip irrigation is necessary to prevent dry periods, which lead to severe fruit cracking.

3. Pest Management:
Pomegranate pest management: Pomegranate Butterfly (Anar Butterfly), Thrips, and Aphids. Butterfly larvae bore into fruits. Bag young fruits or spray spinosad (0.5ml/L) to control.

4. Growth Stages:
Pomegranate has the following stage cycle durations:
- Vegetative Stage: Sowing up to 45 days.
- Flowering Stage: From 45 days to 90 days.
- Fruiting Stage: From 90 days to 150 days.
- Harvesting Stage: From 150 days onwards.

5. Harvesting:
Pomegranate harvesting and post-harvest practices: Harvest fruits when skin turns yellowish-red, becomes hard, and produces a metallic sound when tapped. Avoid pulling fruits; cut using secateurs.

6. Diseases:
Pomegranate diseases: Bacterial Blight (Oily Spot disease) and Wilt. Bacterial blight causes black spots on leaves and fruits; prune affected stems and spray streptocycline (0.5g/L).

7. Soil Requirements:
Pomegranate soil requirements: Deep loam or sandy loam soil with pH 6.0 to 8.0. Tolerates slightly alkaline soil but requires excellent root zone drainage.
