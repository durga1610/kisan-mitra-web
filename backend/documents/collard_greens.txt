=== COLLARD GREENS CROP GUIDE ===

1. Fertilizers:
Collard Greens requires balanced nutrient applications. Recommended dose is 30:20:20 kg of N:P:K per acre.
Category: Leafy Vegetables
- Apply full dose of Phosphorus and Potassium and 50% Nitrogen during sowing/transplanting.
- Apply remaining Nitrogen in two split doses during the active vegetative growth phase.
- Supplement with micronutrient foliar sprays (Iron and Zinc) if chlorosis appears.

2. Irrigation:
Collard Greens irrigation and water requirements: Moderate water requirement. Needs uniform moisture; irrigate every 4-6 days, avoiding waterlogging.

3. Pest Management:
Collard Greens pest management: Common pests affecting Collard Greens are Aphids, Caterpillars, Leaf Miners. Control using neem oil spray or yellow sticky traps.

4. Growth Stages:
Collard Greens has the following stage cycle durations:
- Vegetative Stage: Sowing up to 30 days.
- Flowering Stage: From 30 days to 50 days.
- Fruiting Stage: From 50 days to 70 days.
- Harvesting Stage: From 70 days onwards.

5. Harvesting:
Collard Greens harvesting and post-harvest practices: For Collard Greens, Harvest early in the morning by cutting above soil level. Keep cool and store in well-ventilated crates.

6. Diseases:
Collard Greens diseases: Common diseases affecting Collard Greens are Downy Mildew, Damping-off, Leaf Spot. Spray copper-based fungicides if symptoms appear.

7. Soil Requirements:
Collard Greens soil requirements: Requires well-drained sandy loam soil rich in organic matter. Optimal pH is 6.0 to 7.0.
