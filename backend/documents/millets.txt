=== MILLETS CROP GUIDE ===

1. Fertilizers:
Millets requires balanced nutrient applications. Recommended dose is 20:10:10 kg of N:P:K per acre.
Category: Cereals
- Apply half Nitrogen and full Phosphorus and Potassium as basal.
- Apply remaining Nitrogen at active tillering (30 days after sowing).
- Apply organic compost or vermicompost to enrich marginal soils.

2. Irrigation:
Millets irrigation and water requirements: Low water requirement. Extremely drought-resilient. Requires only 350-500mm of rainfall. Supplementary watering only during flowering if dry spells exceed 3 weeks.

3. Pest Management:
Millets pest management: Shoot Fly and Stem Borer. Shoot fly larvae cause 'dead-hearts' in young seedlings. Sow early and apply neem cake to soil.

4. Growth Stages:
Millets has the following stage cycle durations:
- Vegetative Stage: Sowing up to 30 days.
- Flowering Stage: From 30 days to 60 days.
- Fruiting Stage: From 60 days to 80 days.
- Harvesting Stage: From 80 days onwards.

5. Harvesting:
Millets harvesting and post-harvest practices: Harvest when grains become hard, and moisture drops below 15%. Cut earheads first, thresh, clean, and dry grains to 10-12% moisture for safe storage.

6. Diseases:
Millets diseases: Downy Mildew (Green Ear disease) and Ergot. Ergot causes sticky sugary liquid on earheads, turning dark. Destroy infected earheads and practice crop rotation.

7. Soil Requirements:
Millets soil requirements: Highly resilient crop. Grows in sandy loam, gravelly, or marginal soils with pH 5.5 to 8.0. Sensitive to clay compaction.
