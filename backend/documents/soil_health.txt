Soil Health: Red Soil Characteristics and Treatment
Red Soil is formed by weathering of crystalline and metamorphic rocks. It is rich in potash but deficient in nitrogen, phosphorus, humus, and organic matter. It has a porous, loose structure with high permeability and low water retention.
Treatment: Enhance water retention by adding farmyard manure (FYM), compost, or green manures. Apply lime or calcium carbonate to correct any acidic tendencies. Ideal for groundnuts, millets, cotton, and pulses.

Soil Health: Black Soil (Regur) Characteristics and Treatment
Black Soil is derived from basaltic rocks and is rich in calcium carbonate, potash, and magnesium, but deficient in nitrogen and phosphorus. It has high clay content, swelling when wet and shrinking to form deep cracks when dry. It has excellent water-retention capacity.
Treatment: Regular deep tilling is necessary to prevent compaction. Add organic matter to improve soil aeration. Black soils are ideal for cotton, wheat, sugarcane, linseed, and soybean.

Soil Health: Sandy Soil Characteristics and Treatment
Sandy Soil consists of large particles with plenty of air spaces. Water drains rapidly, taking nutrients with it (leaching). It has low fertility and low organic matter.
Treatment: Apply heavy amounts of compost, vermicompost, or well-rotted manure to build structure and water retention. Use mulching to retain surface moisture. Grow deep-rooting cover crops. Ideal for groundnuts, watermelons, and root vegetables.

Soil Health: Clayey Soil Characteristics and Treatment
Clayey Soil has very fine particles, high water-holding capacity, but poor drainage and aeration. It becomes sticky when wet and extremely hard when dry, making cultivation difficult.
Treatment: Add coarse organic compost, gypsum, or sand to break up clay bonds. Avoid working clayey soil when wet to prevent heavy compaction. Ideal for paddy rice and sorghum.

Soil Health: Alluvial Soil Characteristics and Treatment
Alluvial Soil is highly fertile soil deposited by river waters. It is rich in potash and lime but poor in nitrogen and phosphorus. It has a balanced loamy texture, good drainage, and water retention.
Treatment: Maintain fertility by applying nitrogenous and phosphatic fertilizers or organic compost. Cultivate cover crops to prevent erosion. Highly suited for wheat, rice, sugarcane, jute, and oilseeds.
