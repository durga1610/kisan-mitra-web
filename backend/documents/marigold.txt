=== MARIGOLD CROP GUIDE ===

1. Fertilizers:
Marigold requires balanced nutrient applications. Recommended dose is 40:80:40 kg of N:P:K per acre.
Category: Plantation Crops
- Apply full Phosphorus dose and half of Nitrogen and Potassium as basal.
- Apply the remaining Nitrogen and Potassium 30 days after transplanting.
- Perform pinch-off (removing apical buds) to promote branching and flowering.

2. Irrigation:
Marigold irrigation and water requirements: Low to moderate water requirements. Water weekly. Avoid overhead watering as it causes flower rot and damping-off.

3. Pest Management:
Marigold pest management: Red Spider Mites and Aphids. Mites cause leaf yellowing and webbing. Spray wettable sulphur (2g/L) to control mites.

4. Growth Stages:
Marigold has the following stage cycle durations:
- Vegetative Stage: Sowing up to 30 days.
- Flowering Stage: From 30 days to 60 days.
- Fruiting Stage: From 60 days to 80 days.
- Harvesting Stage: From 80 days onwards.

5. Harvesting:
Marigold harvesting and post-harvest practices: Harvest fully opened flowers when they achieve mature size. Harvest in mornings. Store in ventilated crates and keep cool.

6. Diseases:
Marigold diseases: Damping-off of seedlings, Alternaria Leaf Spot, and Flower Bud Rot. Bud rot causes brown discoloration. Spray copper-based fungicides to control.

7. Soil Requirements:
Marigold soil requirements: Well-drained loamy or sandy loam soil with pH 7.0 to 7.5. Tolerates a wide variety of soils but avoid waterlogged clay zones.
