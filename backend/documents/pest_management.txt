Pest Management: Cotton Bollworm (Helicoverpa armigera)
Symptoms: Caterpillars bore holes into square buds, flowers, and bolls. Eaten buds show flares. Dropping of squares and bolls is common.
Organic Action: Spray Neem seed kernel extract (NSKE 5%) or apply Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt) formulation. Set up pheromone traps (5-10 per acre).
Chemical Action: Spray Chlorantraniliprole (Coragen) at 0.4 ml per liter or Spinosad at 0.3 ml per liter of water.
Best Practices: Plant trap crops like Marigold around cotton fields. Hand-pick and destroy early-stage caterpillars.

Pest Management: Whiteflies (Bemisia tabaci)
Symptoms: Nymphs and adults suck sap from the underside of leaves, causing yellowing, leaf crumpling, and shedding. Honeydew secretion leads to sooty mold growth. Vector for Cotton Leaf Curl Virus.
Organic Action: Spray Neem Oil (3000 ppm) at 5 ml per liter with soap solution. Use yellow sticky traps (15-20 per acre).
Chemical Action: Spray Diafenthiuron (Pegasus) at 1 gram per liter or Acetamiprid at 0.2 grams per liter.
Best Practices: Avoid excess nitrogenous fertilizer which attracts sucking pests. Maintain weed-free field borders.

Pest Management: Stem Borer in Paddy Rice (Scirpophaga incertulas)
Symptoms: Dead hearts (drying of central leaf whorl) in vegetative stage and whiteheads (empty, chaffy panicles) in reproductive stage.
Organic Action: Use Trichogramma japonicum egg parasitoid cards at 2-3 per acre. Set up light traps to capture moths.
Chemical Action: Apply Cartap Hydrochloride 4G granules at 10 kg per acre or spray Chlorantraniliprole at 0.4 ml per liter.
Best Practices: Clip leaf tips of seedlings before transplanting to destroy stem borer eggs. Harvest close to the ground.

Pest Management: Tomato Fruit Borer
Symptoms: Larva bores into tomato fruits making them unfit for consumption. Secondary rot pathogens enter the bored holes.
Organic Action: Spray Helicoverpa armigera Nuclear Polyhedrosis Virus (HaNPV) or Bacillus thuringiensis. Install pheromone traps.
Chemical Action: Spray Flubendiamide or Spinosad.
Best Practices: Intercrop with Marigold as a trap crop (1 row of marigold for every 14 rows of tomato).
