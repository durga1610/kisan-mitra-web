Harvesting Practices: Paddy Rice
Maturity Indicators: The grains in the panicle turn golden yellow (about 80-85% of grains), and moisture content drops to 20-22%. Senescence (yellowing) of lower leaves is visible.
Harvesting: Cut plants close to the ground (10-15 cm) manually or using mechanical reapers.
Post-Harvest: Thresh the harvested crop immediately to separate grain from straw. Dry the grains under shade or in dryers to achieve a safe storage moisture content of 14% to prevent mold and insect attack.

Harvesting Practices: Tomato
Maturity Indicators: Tomatoes do not ripen all at once. Harvest according to the market distance: 'Green wrap' stage for distant markets (mature but green), 'Pink' or 'Breaker' stage (slight pink at blossom end) for local markets, and 'Red ripe' for immediate consumption or processing.
Harvesting: Harvest individually by twisting fruits gently with stems attached to maintain freshness. Best done in early morning.
Post-Harvest: Sort fruits by size, maturity, and quality. Place in well-ventilated plastic crates. Store at 12-15°C; do not refrigerate under-ripe tomatoes.

Harvesting Practices: Cotton
Maturity Indicators: Bolls mature and burst open, revealing clean white lint. Avoid picking semi-opened bolls.
Harvesting: Harvest (pick) manually in dry weather after the morning dew has dried to prevent staining and moisture buildup in stored cotton.
Post-Harvest: Sort and separate stained, damaged cotton from clean white cotton. Store in clean, dry, well-ventilated rooms on elevated wooden platforms to avoid moisture absorption.

Harvesting Practices: Potato
Maturity Indicators: Vines turn yellow and dry up naturally. The skin of tubers becomes firm and does not easily rub off when pressed.
Harvesting: Stop irrigation 10-14 days before harvest. Cut the above-ground vines (dehalming) 7-10 days before harvesting to allow the tuber skin to mature and harden. Dig up carefully to avoid bruising.
Post-Harvest: Cure the tubers by storing them in a cool (15-18°C), dark, and humid (85% RH) room for 7-10 days to heal injuries. Store in cold storage at 4°C for seed or table potatoes.
