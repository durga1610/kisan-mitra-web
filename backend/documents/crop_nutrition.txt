Crop Nutrition: Nitrogen Deficiency
Symptoms: Pale green to light yellowing of older leaves (chlorosis) starting from the leaf tips and margins, progressing towards the base. Plant growth is severely stunted, stems become thin and woody, and crop maturity may be delayed or reduced.
Correction: Apply Urea as a top dressing, or spray 1-2% Urea solution on foliage for quick absorption. Apply compost or nitrogen-fixing biofertilizers for long-term health.

Crop Nutrition: Phosphorus Deficiency
Symptoms: Dark green leaves that gradually develop purple or reddish-bronze tints, particularly on the veins and lower sides of older leaves. Root growth is poor, flowering and maturity are delayed, and fruit/grain development is restricted.
Correction: Apply Single Super Phosphate (SSP) or Diammonium Phosphate (DAP) close to the root zone. Maintain soil pH between 6.0 and 7.0 to improve phosphorus availability.

Crop Nutrition: Potassium Deficiency
Symptoms: Yellowing and necrosis (burning/browning) of the leaf margins and tips of older leaves, often referred to as leaf scorch. Stalks become weak and prone to lodging. Fruit size is small, quality is poor, and disease susceptibility increases.
Correction: Apply Muriate of Potash (MOP) or Potassium Sulfate to the soil. Use foliar sprays of Potassium Nitrate (1%) for rapid action during fruit development.

Crop Nutrition: Zinc Deficiency
Symptoms: Interveinal chlorosis (yellowing between leaf veins) on young leaves. Stunted growth, shorter internodes leading to a rosette appearance (bunchy leaf tops), and bronze spots on older leaves. In rice, it causes the "Khaira" disease characterized by rusty brown spots on leaves.
Correction: Apply Zinc Sulfate (21% Zn) at 10-15 kg per acre during soil preparation. Spray Zinc Chelates (0.5%) on leaves for immediate treatment.

Crop Nutrition: Iron and Boron Deficiencies
Iron Deficiency: Pronounced ivory-white or pale-yellow coloring between veins of youngest upper leaves. Correct by spraying Ferrous Sulfate (0.5%).
Boron Deficiency: Death of growing tips, hollow stems in broccoli/cabbage, poor pollination, and cracked/hollow fruit or tubers in tomato and potato. Correct by applying Borax (2-4 kg/acre) or spraying Boron (0.1-0.2%).
