=== GRAPE CROP GUIDE ===

1. Fertilizers:
Grape requires balanced nutrient applications. Recommended dose is 50:50:100 kg of N:P:K per acre.
Category: Fruit Crops
- Apply full dose of Phosphorus and Potassium during pruning.
- Apply Nitrogen in split doses: 50% post-pruning and 50% during berry growth.
- Apply Boron and Zinc foliar sprays before flowering to improve fruit set.

2. Irrigation:
Grape irrigation and water requirements: Moderate water requirements. Drip irrigation is highly recommended. Critical watering stages are bud burst, flowering, and berry development. Avoid waterlogging and excessive humidity to prevent fungal infections.

3. Pest Management:
Grape pest management: Mealybugs, Thrips, and Flea beetles are common. Thrips feed on blossoms causing scarred berries. Control using yellow sticky traps and spraying neem oil or spinosad.

4. Growth Stages:
Grape has the following stage cycle durations:
- Vegetative Stage: Sowing up to 45 days.
- Flowering Stage: From 45 days to 90 days.
- Fruiting Stage: From 90 days to 120 days.
- Harvesting Stage: From 120 days onwards.

5. Harvesting:
Grape harvesting and post-harvest practices: Harvest when berries are fully ripe, sweet, and uniform in color. Avoid harvesting after rain. Keep in cold storage at 0-2 degrees Celsius with 90% relative humidity to prolong shelf life.

6. Diseases:
Grape diseases: Downy Mildew, Powdery Mildew, Anthracnose, and Black Rot. Downy Mildew causes white cottony growth on the leaf underside; control by spraying copper oxychloride (3g/L) or systemic fungicides.

7. Soil Requirements:
Grape soil requirements: Well-drained sandy loam or gravelly clay loam soil with pH between 6.5 and 7.5. Avoid heavy soils with poor drainage and high salt content.
